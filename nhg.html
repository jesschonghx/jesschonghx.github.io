About

As part of Singapore’ s national initiative, Healthier Sg, the Partners’ portal was developed. It aims to connect residents to community care and centralise programme management for NHG’s (National Healthcare Group) community partners. Through the Partners’ portal, community partners can upload their programmes which are accessible by residents via the NHG Cares App.

Gathering Requirements

To understand the requirements, we held meetings with NHG users to explore their structure, needs, and challenges. While NHG users wanted tools to share and visualise information, community partners needed to track attendance, view data, and issue certificates.

Using FigJam, we mapped out user flows based on these insights, aligning with stakeholders before starting the design phase.

Getting things off the ground

To ensure that we were on the right path, we explored apps in the fitness industry with Healthy365, ClassPass, and TeamUp identified as potential benchmarks. Due to limited access to Healthy365's vendor application, we focused on ClassPass and TeamUp.

A heuristic evaluation was conducted based on collected screenshots to assess their usability and features. We then applied what we thought would be feasible for our project.

Relying on the Second Brain

Information can accumulate up quickly when dealing with multiple stakeholders from NHG HQ and hospitals as well as the community partners. As such documentation was crucial to help us manage our information, and refresh our memory when needed. Minutes was done on Notion, with emphasis on follow up actions and who should follow up on them.

Wireframe

After mapping out the user flows and aligning them with the stakeholders, we started working on high fidelity wireframes.

“Clean and Straightforward”

Was what one of the users told me when I was conducting user acceptance testing on the portal. The portal was designed to have a clean and simple interface, with the primary teal colour in line with NHG’s brand guideline. The design aimed to make it easy for users to find what they need and quickly mark attendance during sessions.

Learnings

Working on this project gave me a firsthand experience of collaborating with external stakeholders, which was both challenging and rewarding. The tight timeline meant we couldn’t conduct user testing beforehand, but hearing feedback after the portal’s implementation was a learning experience that helped me grow and refine my approach for future projects.
